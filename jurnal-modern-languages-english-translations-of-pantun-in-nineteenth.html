Passengers who understand English = 150

Passengers who understand French = 122

To find the number of passengers who cannot understand either English or French, we can subtract the number of passengers who understand both languages from the total number of passengers:

Number of passengers who understand neither English nor French = Total passengers - (Passengers who understand English + Passengers who understand French)

= 320 - (150 + 122)

= 320 - 272

= 48

So, there are 48 passengers who cannot understand either English or French.

To find the percentage, we can divide the number of passengers who cannot understand either language by the total number of passengers and multiply by 100:

Percentage of passengers who cannot understand either English or French = (Number of passengers who cannot understand either language / Total passengers) x 100

= (48 / 320) x 100

= 15%

Therefore, the percentage of passengers who cannot understand either English or French is 15%.
