11. A Noble Gangster There was a time when the owners of shops and businesses in Chicago had pay large sums of money to gangsters in return for 'potection'. If the money was not paid promptly, the gangsters would quickly put a man out of business by destroying his shop. Obtaining 'potection money' is not a modern crime. As long ago as the fourteenth century, an Englishman, Sir John Hawkwood, made the remarkable discovery that people would rather pay large sums of money than have their life work destroyed by gangsters.Six hundred years ago, Sir John Hawkwood arrived in Italy with a band of soldiers and settled near Florence. He soon made a name for himself and came to be known to the Italians as Giovanni Acuto. Whenever the Italian city-states were at war with each other, Hawkwood used to hire his soldiers to princes who were willing to pay the high price he demanded. in times of peace, when business was bad, Hawkwood and his men would march into a city-state and, after burning down a few farms, would offer to go away if pretection money was paid to them. Hawkwood made large sums of money in this way. In spite of this, the Italians regarded him as a sort of hero. When he ided at the age of eighty, the Florentines gave him a state funeral and had a picture painted which was dedicated to the memory of 'the most valiant soldier and most notable leader, Signor Giovanni Haukodue'Question1.who was sir john hawkwood? Jawaban:

Seorang Gangster Mulia

Ada suatu masa ketika para pemilik toko dan bisnis di Chicago membayar sejumlah besar uang kepada gangster dengan imbalan 'ramuan'. Jika uang itu tidak segera dibayarkan, para gangster akan segera membuat seseorang gulung tikar dengan menghancurkan tokonya. Mendapatkan 'uang pot' bukanlah kejahatan modern. Pada abad keempat belas yang lalu, seorang Inggris, Sir John Hawkwood, membuat penemuan luar biasa bahwa orang lebih suka membayar sejumlah besar uang daripada pekerjaan hidup mereka dihancurkan oleh gangster.

Enam ratus tahun yang lalu, Sir John Hawkwood tiba di Italia dengan sekelompok tentara dan menetap di dekat Florence. Dia segera membuat nama untuk dirinya sendiri dan kemudian dikenal oleh orang Italia sebagai Giovanni Acuto. Setiap kali negara-kota Italia berperang satu sama lain, Hawkwood biasa mempekerjakan tentaranya kepada para pangeran yang bersedia membayar harga tinggi yang dia minta. di masa damai, ketika bisnis sedang buruk, Hawkwood dan anak buahnya akan berbaris ke negara kota dan, setelah membakar beberapa pertanian, akan menawarkan untuk pergi jika uang palsu dibayarkan kepada mereka. Hawkwood menghasilkan banyak uang dengan cara ini. Meskipun demikian, orang Italia menganggapnya sebagai pahlawan. Ketika dia memohon pada usia delapan puluh, Florentines memberinya pemakaman kenegaraan dan melukis gambar yang didedikasikan untuk mengenang 'prajurit paling gagah berani dan pemimpin paling terkenal, Signor Giovanni Haukodue'
